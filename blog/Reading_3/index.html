Reading 3

  • 18 March 2026

Reflection

What I keep coming back to after these readings is how much the web changes things without it being obvious. It is easy to think of it as a tool that just delivers information, but all three texts make it clear that it is constantly reshaping what we see and how we understand it.

With Chimero’s idea of the web having a kind of “grain,” it made me think about how often design ignores that and tries to force things into formats that feel more controlled or familiar. There is something interesting about the idea that the web is not neutral or empty, but has its own logic that affects how things move and feel. It makes design feel less like total control and more like working within something that already has structure.

That connects in a strange way to the idea in Chiang’s piece, where information gets compressed into something more general. The “blurry JPEG” idea really stuck with me, because it feels like a good way to describe not just AI, but a lot of online experience in general. Things are simplified so they can be processed more easily, but that also means losing detail, context, and sometimes meaning. It made me think about how often I rely on summaries, feeds, or generated content without really questioning what is being left out.

The reading about digital memory adds another layer to that. If information is constantly being stored, retrieved, and reshaped by systems, then memory itself starts to feel less personal and more structured by the platforms we use. It is not just about remembering things, but about how those things are organized and accessed later. That changes the relationship between people and information in a way that feels subtle but significant.

What feels most interesting to me is that none of these changes are immediately visible. They are built into the system in a way that feels natural over time. The more you use these technologies, the less you notice how they are shaping your behavior or your understanding. That makes it harder to question them, even though they are constantly influencing what feels true or important.

Overall, these readings made me more aware of how much interpretation is happening behind the scenes. The web is not just a space where information exists. It is something that actively transforms it, whether through design, compression, or storage. That makes it feel less like a neutral medium and more like something that is always filtering and reshaping what we experience.
